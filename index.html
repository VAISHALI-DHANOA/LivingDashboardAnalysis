Living Dashboard Study — Results Explorer

HCI study comparing an AI-driven Living Dashboard with a static Baseline dashboard

Study Design & Methodology. This between-subjects study compared two dashboard conditions with 12 participants (6 per condition).
Living Dashboard — participants L1–L6 — featured an AI-driven interface that automatically resized, dimmed, and rearranged views based on user activity.
Baseline — participants B1–B6 — provided a conventional static dashboard.

Scoring: SUS uses a 1–5 Likert scale across 10 items; all negative items were pre-reverse-coded, so the formula is Σ(item−1)×2.5, yielding 0–100. NASA-TLX items use a 1–7 scale; the workload aggregate is the mean of Mental Demand, Effort, and Annoyance (excluding Success). Satisfaction and LD-specific items use 1–7 Likert scales. The comparative item uses a −2 (much worse) to +2 (much better) scale.

SUS Scores

System Usability Scale (SUS) score per participant. Each of the 10 items (1–5 Likert) was adjusted by subtracting 1, summed, and multiplied by 2.5 to yield a 0–100 score. All negative items were pre-reverse-coded. Dashed lines show condition means; background bands mark standard SUS benchmarks.

NASA-TLX

Task workload rated on a 1–7 scale across four NASA-TLX subscales. For Mental Demand, Effort, and Annoyance lower scores are better; for Success higher is better. Error bars show ±1 SD.

Overall & AI Satisfaction

Single-item satisfaction ratings on a 1–7 Likert scale. "Overall" measures general dashboard satisfaction; "AI" measures perceived quality of AI-generated suggestions. Error bars show ±1 SD.

Living Dashboard-Specific Items

Nine Likert items (1–7) rated only by Living Dashboard participants, capturing perceived quality of adaptive behaviours. Sorted by mean descending. The highest-variance item is highlighted with a dashed border. Error bars show ±1 SD.

Comparative Item (Living Dashboard Only)

Each Living Dashboard participant rated how the adaptive dashboard compared to a conventional static dashboard on a −2 (much worse) to +2 (much better) scale. The diamond shows the group mean.
